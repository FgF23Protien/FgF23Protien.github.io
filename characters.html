Jefferius Eptien

Chosen one, Hunnic Warrior King

Roman Catholic Born 360AD

Jefferius Eptein may stand only four feet, eleven inches tall, but his diminutive stature and herculean build belies a terrifying presence that commands absolute obedience across the steppes. A power-hungry tactician with a mind as sharp as a Mongol saber, Jefferius is renowned for a cunning that allows him to dismantle much larger imperial armies before a single arrow is even loosed. His most striking feature, a perfectly groomed and intimidatingly wide handlebar mustache, serves as a symbol of his vanity and iron-fisted rule. Under his command are the world’s most elite horseback archers, a force of unrivaled speed and precision capable of turning the horizon into a wall of whistling death. Whether he is orchestrating a complex pincer movement or personally leading a charge, Jefferius Eptein remains a ruthless visionary whose ambition knows no borders and whose enemies are invariably left in the dust of his wake. Though he still appreciates the finer things in life like poetry, beer, education, beer, music, beer, literature, beer, theatre, beer, puzzles, and more beer. A horseman, and archer paralleled by none. Known widely among his circles of influence for his affinity for training and commanding animals from horses to elephants.

Bradford Dekari

Commander

Zorostrain Born 340AD

In the year 377 AD, as the long shadow of the Roman East stretched thin across its distant provinces, the Sasanian court in Ctesiphon placed its confidence in an ambitious commander known as Bradford. Though not born among the oldest noble houses of Persia, Bradford had earned renown along the empire’s western marches for his discipline, patience, and an unusual ability to wage war as much with logistics and diplomacy as with steel. Appointed to lead a campaign toward the rich Roman province of Syria, he saw the region not merely as a prize of conquest but as the keystone of influence over the eastern Mediterranean. From fortified camps along the Euphrates he assembled a careful advance—cataphracts drawn from noble cavalry, hardened infantry from Mesopotamia, and engineers skilled in siegecraft—while agents spread quiet promises among discontented cities weary of Roman taxation and distant emperors. Bradford believed that Syria would not fall by a single great battle but by the slow tightening of a vice: cutting Roman supply routes, isolating garrisons, and presenting himself not as a destroyer, but as the inevitable new master of the province. Among his officers it was said that he studied the maps of Antioch and the Orontes valley late into the night, convinced that whoever commanded Syria would command the fate of the frontier itself.

Arenicos Skorpii

Emperor

Christian Orthodox Born 360AD

14 years old. Arenicos was only 14 years old when his father, Commodus, a noble man who lead some of Rome's greatest legions, was slain in battle. Vengeance. Arenicos demanded vengeance. Vengeance against Maximus, the snake who took the life of his father. Vengeance, vengeance against the Empire that dares to label itself as glorius. Vengeance, against himself, for not being at his fathers side on the field of battle. Born to a noble family of Roman soldiers, Arenicos was quick to learn the realities of war and politics. Being on the front, Arenicos learned to ride into his foes as a hammer strikes an anvil. Surrounded by cavalry, Arenicos dreams to live up to his Father, who was Romes greatest cavalry commander. Life on the road with his father has taught him how to survive, not just in the open plain but how to survive with people, how to socialize. Granted the title of Governor promised to his late father, Arenicos bides his time, masking his intrigue with civil duties. "O care pater, non morieris frustra. Ad bellum! Ad bellum contra omnes hostes Scorpii!"

Pholus Mucianus

Merchant

Christianity Born 351AD

Pholus came from a family of prominent merchants, he has multiple profitable enterprises after the un-timely demise of his late father Phanes Mucianus. As the eldest son, he inhereted, by the will of his father, a profitable trade fleet. An avid sailor since he was young, he is the captian of his trade fleet and commands the fleet throughout their trading voyage. Pholus has an estate in Syria where he resides when not sailing with his fleet. His mother died in a navel raid 15 years ago, in 362. His grandfather, a crazy old guy proclaimed 'The Wise Man of Athens' has rarely ever interacted with Pholus. His wife, a skilled in stewardship, manages his estate while he is away. His younger brother was too young to inherit the family trade buisness, and remains somewhat bitter about that, though still works alongside Pholus in his fleet, learning the art of trade so that he too may someday own a trade fleet.

Frederickus Barnus

General

Christianity Born 344AD

His father's father welcomed the Roman Legions as liberators when they conquered Brittania. They brought civilization, peace, stability, to a lawless world. Fredirckus gladly answered the call to serve his empire, remembering the patriotic fever of his ancestors. And now as the barbarians march on the gates again, they dare to destroy everything the empire built? No, the Barnus dynasty worked too hard, and has sacrificed too much to let that happen. Fredrickus would rather die then let Rome fall to such men.
